Opera 3.60

Version 3.60 · 2000

A tiny, fast, fiercely independent browser from Norway that invented features the giants spent years catching up to.

A short history

Opera began in 1994 as a research project at Telenor, Norway's telecom, and spun out as Opera Software in Oslo. For years it was paid, ad-supported software with a reputation for speed and standards support — even on modest hardware. The 3.x era cemented a small but intensely loyal following.

Opera never had the market share of Netscape or IE, but it had something better: a habit of inventing the future first.

Lesser-known & curious

  • It fit on a floppy disk. The entire browser was famously small — well under 1.5 MB — at a time when rivals ballooned to tens of megabytes.
  • It pioneered the modern browser. Tabbed browsing, mouse gestures, page zoom and saved sessions all appeared in Opera long before the competition.
  • It carried an ad. For years a small banner sat in the toolbar; Opera only became fully free and ad-free in 2005.
  • Its M2 mail client organized messages with labels and "access points" instead of folders — radical for its day.
  • It was obsessive about standards and accessibility — full keyboard control, and even voice navigation.
  • Countless "new" ideas in other browsers — speed dial, tab previews — were Opera's first.
