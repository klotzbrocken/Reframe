Internet Explorer 3.02

Version 3.02 · 1997
March 1997 · Windows 95

The first Internet Explorer that could really fight — and the one that made the blue “e” famous.

A short history

Internet Explorer 3.0 arrived in August 1996 (3.02 followed in early 1997) and was Microsoft’s first browser that could genuinely stand up to Netscape. It was largely rewritten, introduced support for CSS, Java, frames and ActiveX, and bundled Internet Mail & News — while Microsoft, unlike Netscape, gave it away completely free.

It was also the debut of the blue “e” logo that would represent “the internet” for a generation. Within a year, IE’s market share jumped from low single digits to roughly 20–30% — the browser war was on.

Lesser-known & curious

  • One of the first CSS browsers ever. IE3 shipped commercial CSS support before Netscape did — months before the CSS1 spec was even final.
  • The Netscape doormat prank. After IE 3.0 launched, Microsoft employees planted a giant “e” logo on Netscape’s front lawn overnight. Netscape toppled it and put their Mozilla dinosaur mascot on top, holding a sign: “Netscape 72, Microsoft 18” — the market shares.
  • Hot-tracking toolbar. The toolbar icons were grayscale until the mouse passed over them and they lit up in colour — recreated faithfully in this theme.
  • The spinning “e”. The 46-frame animated throbber in the black box (top right) is the original artwork from shdocvw.dll.
  • ActiveX was its superpower and its curse — powerful native components in the page, and one of the biggest security headaches of the decade.
  • IE3 also introduced Favorites folders and the quick-launch “Links” bar that survived all the way to IE11.
