Netscape Communicator 4.7

Version 4.7 · 1999
1999 · Windows 9x

Netscape didn't just make a browser — it made the web a place ordinary people wanted to be.

A short history

Descended from Mosaic, Netscape Navigator popularized the graphical web and by the mid-1990s held around 90% of the market. The Communicator 4.x suite bundled the browser with Mail & News, an address book, and Composer, a WYSIWYG page editor. Netscape's 1995 IPO — a company with little revenue and no profit — soared on day one and helped ignite the entire dot-com boom.

By 1999 the tide was turning as Microsoft bundled Internet Explorer with Windows. But Netscape's fingerprints are still all over the web you use today.

Lesser-known & curious

  • "Mozilla" = "Mosaic killer". Netscape's internal codename and dinosaur mascot — coined by engineer Jamie Zawinski — live on in Firefox to this day.
  • It gave us JavaScript. Brendan Eich wrote the first version in about ten days in 1995.
  • It invented the cookie (Lou Montulli, 1994) and SSL, the encryption that still secures the web (now TLS).
  • It also created the much-mocked <blink> tag — reportedly dreamed up over beers.
  • The animated throbber — the shooting stars and spinning "N" in the corner — was Netscape's way of showing a page was loading.
  • Its aging engine was so hard to fix that Netscape threw it out and rewrote from scratch — later called the worst strategic mistake in software in Joel Spolsky's essay "Things You Should Never Do".
