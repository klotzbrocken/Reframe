Firefox 1.0

Version 1.0 · 2004
November 2004 · cross-platform

A scrappy open-source browser, funded by its own users, broke the most entrenched monopoly in software.

A short history

Firefox rose from the ashes of Netscape via the Mozilla project. A few developers, frustrated by the bloated all-in-one Mozilla suite, spun off a lean, standalone browser. Version 1.0 landed in November 2004 with tabbed browsing, a pop-up blocker and add-on extensions, and it rapidly clawed market share back from Internet Explorer — reopening a web that had nearly stopped competing.

Within a year it had been downloaded over 100 million times, proving that an open, community-driven browser could take on the giants and win.

Lesser-known & curious

  • The name was a saga. It started as Phoenix (rising from Netscape's ashes), hit a trademark clash and became Firebird, clashed with a database of the same name, and finally landed on Firefox.
  • A "firefox" isn't a fox. It's another name for the red panda — the animal in the logo.
  • Users bought the launch ad. To celebrate 1.0, the community crowdfunded a two-page ad in The New York Times listing the names of more than 10,000 donors.
  • Its extension model — letting anyone reshape the browser — spawned an entire ecosystem and inspired every browser since.
  • The "Mozilla" name and the dinosaur mascot trace straight back to Netscape in 1994.
  • Firefox's success is what eventually pushed Microsoft to start improving Internet Explorer again.
