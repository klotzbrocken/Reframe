Netscape Communicator 6.0

Version 6.0 · 2000
November 2000 · Windows

The most important browser Netscape ever shipped was also one of its most criticized.

A short history

After famously abandoning its old Navigator code, Netscape rebuilt everything on a brand-new engine called Gecko. Netscape 6.0, released in November 2000 under AOL's ownership, was the first product of that rewrite and introduced the skinnable "Modern" interface. But it shipped before the underlying Mozilla code was truly ready.

Reviewers and longtime fans found it slow and unstable next to the old 4.7 — yet the engine it debuted would quietly outlive nearly every browser of its generation.

Lesser-known & curious

  • There was no Netscape 5. The version jumped straight from 4 to 6 — partly to leapfrog Internet Explorer 5.
  • It was built on a pre-1.0 engine. 6.0 was based on an early Mozilla milestone, which is why it felt so rough at launch.
  • It introduced XUL-based themes — you could reskin the entire browser, an idea Firefox later refined.
  • The animated "N" throbber returned, now spinning over a silver "Modern" toolbar.
  • Gecko, its engine, still powers Firefox today — more than two decades later.
  • In a twist, AOL — which owned Netscape — kept paying to keep Internet Explorer as the default inside its own AOL software.
